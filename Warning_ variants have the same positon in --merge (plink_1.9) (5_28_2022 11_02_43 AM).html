Hello Chris,

It seems I am facing some new merge issues.

I plan to merge two filesets taking only the common snps between the two datasets. I followed the following steps

Data1= PlinkQC1.map, PlinkQC1.ped

Data2=PlinkQC2.map, PlinkQC2.ped

 

1)EXTRACTING COMMON SNPs between the two data-sets

> R code:

> map2 = read.delim("PlinkQC2.map", header=F, quote="")

> map1 = read.delim("PlinkQC1.map", header=F, quote="")

> common.snps = which(map2$V2 %in% map1$V2)

> write.table(map2$V2[common.snps], file="list.snps", sep="\t", col.names=F, row.names=F, quote=F )

The above steps generated a list of common snps between the two datasets “list.snps” text file.

My list.snps had = 389589 snps

2)Converted the map/ped files to binary (bed/bim/fam) for each dataset #plink 1.9

Data1= PlinkCleanQC1.bed/PlinkCleanQC1.bim/PlinkCleanQC1.fam

Data2= PlinkCleanQC2.bed/PlinkCleanQC2.bim/PlinkCleanQC2.fam

3)Extract list.snps (common snps) from both data set.

> plink --bfile <Data1> --extract list.snps --make-bed --out data1

389589 snps extracted written to Clean1list binary file

> plink --bfile <Data2> --extract list.snps --make-bed --out data2

389589 snps extracted and written Clean2list binary file

4)Removing non-acgt snp

>plink2 --bfile Clean1list --snps-only just-acgt --make-bed --out Clean1list_biallelic

388821 out of 389589 passed the filter

>plink2 --bfile Clean2list --snps-only just-acgt --make-bed --out Clean2list_biallelic

388821 out of 389589 passed the filter NOTE: it seems strange, both filesets have equal number of non-acgt snps

5)Removing non-biallelic snps (though  step 4 might have already removed them)

>plink2 --bfile Clean1list_biallelic --max-alleles 2 --make-bed --out Clean1listbi_biallelic

As expected all were removed by previous steps

>plink2 --bfile Clean1list_biallelic --max-alleles 2 --make-bed --out Clean1listbi_biallelic

As expected all were removed by previous steps

6)Removing duplicate snps in each dataset

>plink2 --bfile Clean1listbi_biallelic --set-all-var-ids @:#[b37]\$r,\$a --rm-dup force-first  --make-bed --out Clean1bibi_dup

No duplicates were there

>plink2 --bfile Clean2listbi_biallelic --set-all-var-ids @:#[b37]\$r,\$a --rm-dup force-first  --make-bed --out Clean2bibi_dup

No duplicates were there

7)Merging the two datasets #plink/1.90

plink --bfile Clean1bibi_dup --bmerge Clean2bibi_dup --make-bed --out merge1

Merge happens BUT many warnings regarding variants sharing same positions (screenshot1)

When I inspect the merge1.log file manually, I see that most if not all of the variants are duplicates (screenshot2 attached) which is strange because I thought step 6 above took care of that. Now, for this merge step, plink doesn’t give me the list of offending snps in a separate file, otherwise I could have excluded them from the files and remerged.

I am not sure where I have faulted and how to proceed further. Looking at the merge1.log file,  I feel like excluding all the snps with missing values ie (.) in both the pre-merge filesets, but the approach seems highly conservative/restrictive.

I also tried --merge-equal-pos, with step 7, but errors out

Would highly appreciate if you could please provide some feedback.

Okay, the missing minor-allele codes in the original datasets do hurt you here.  Since it's possible for the major alleles to be mismatched between your two datasets (e.g. the frequency of an allele could be ~49% in one dataset and ~51% in the other), I guess the best --set-all-var-ids template for this purpose has only chromosome and position; the missing-backslash "bug" in your initial choice inadvertently caused the right thing to happen.

You may still get some merge errors, but they'll correspond to multiallelic SNPs that it's reasonable to filter out.
